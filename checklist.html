The 27-Point Cold Email Checklist

The exact pre-send checklist I run before every campaign. It's how I lifted reply rates 30%+ and beat a $350K quota. Run every campaign through it and watch your replies change.

1 The List (where 80% of campaigns die)

  • Your ICP is written down: industry, company size, geography and exact job titles. If you can't state who it's for, the email is for no one.
  • Every account was researched before it went on the list, not scraped blind.
  • One clearly-identified decision-maker per company. No info@ addresses.
  • Every email is verified. Target under 2% bounce rate or your domain reputation pays for it.
  • A suppression list exists: current clients, competitors, past opt-outs.
  • Each row is enriched with at least one personalization hook: a trigger event, a hire, funding, a post they wrote.

2 Deliverability (invisible until it kills you)

  • You send from a secondary domain (e.g. tryyourbrand.com), never your main company domain.
  • SPF, DKIM and DMARC records are set and verified.
  • The inbox was warmed for 2 to 3 weeks before real volume.
  • Volume stays under ~50 cold sends per inbox per day.
  • First-touch emails contain no links, no images, no attachments.
  • Copy avoids spam triggers: FREE, guarantee, act now, excessive caps or exclamation marks.

3 The Copy (relevance beats cleverness)

  • Subject line is 1 to 4 words, lowercase, curiosity-driven. It looks like a colleague wrote it.
  • The first line is about THEM, never "My name is..." They decide in one line whether to keep reading.
  • The email names one specific pain point your ICP actually has.
  • There's one proof point with a real number ("lifted reply rates 30%+", "beat a $350K quota").
  • Exactly one call to action, phrased as a low-friction question ("Worth a 15-minute chat?").
  • Total length is under 90 words. It must read comfortably on a phone.
  • Read it aloud: if it doesn't sound like a human talking, rewrite it.

4 The Sequence (the money is in the follow-up)

  • The sequence has 4 to 6 touches spread over about 3 weeks. Most replies come on touches 2 to 4.
  • Every follow-up adds something new: an insight, a case study, a different angle. Never "just bumping this".
  • There's a polite breakup email at the end. It routinely gets the highest reply rate.
  • A LinkedIn touch (view, connect or comment) is layered between emails for multi-channel presence.

5 Metrics & Iteration (what separates pros)

  • Opens, replies and meetings are tracked weekly, in a CRM or a sheet, not from memory.
  • You A/B test exactly one variable at a time: subject OR first line OR CTA.
  • Reply rate under 5%? Fix the list and relevance first. Copy is rarely the real problem.
  • Positive replies get a meeting link the same day. Speed to meeting is a conversion rate of its own.
